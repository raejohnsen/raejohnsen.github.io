Marie Antoinette

The film is known for its visually striking and sumptuous cinematography. The color palette is rich and opulent, reflecting the extravagant lifestyle of the French court during the late 18th century. The use of pastel and vibrant colors, particularly in costumes and set design, creates a visually captivating and lush atmosphere.
Colors are used symbolically to represent different aspects of Marie Antoinette's life and the society in which she lived. For example, the opulent gold and blue colors are associated with the French monarchy, while the pink and pastel colors represent Marie Antoinette's personal and frivolous world.
The color scheme in the film is used to set emotional tones and moods. For example, the use of pastels and bright colors in the earlier parts of the film reflects the carefree and indulgent lifestyle of the court, while the color palette becomes darker and more somber as the film progresses and the French Revolution looms.
The film's use of color is more focused on creating a stylized and artistic interpretation of Marie Antoinette's life rather than striving for strict historical accuracy. It places an emphasis on aesthetics and storytelling rather than strict adherence to historical records.
